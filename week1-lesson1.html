Main Content (Deborah-Led)

Reading (15 minutes)

The Australian Constitution was created when the six British colonies decided to federate into one nation. It was passed by the United Kingdom Parliament and came into effect on 1 January 1901. The Constitution establishes the federal system, dividing legislative powers between the Commonwealth Parliament and the State Parliaments. It sets up the three branches of government: the Parliament (which makes laws), the Executive (which administers the laws), and the Judiciary (which interprets the laws). This system is often called the 'Washminster' system because it combines elements of the British Westminster system (responsible government) and the American Washington system (federal structure and written constitution) (Australian Government, n.d.; Parliamentary Education Office, n.d.).
