Reading: The Road to Federation (15 minutes)

In the late 19th century, Australia was six separate British colonies. Each had its own parliament, laws, and defence forces. Leaders like Sir Henry Parkes (the Father of Federation) pushed for unity. His Tenterfield Oration in 1889 called for one national government. Conventions in 1890 and 1891, led by Edmund Barton, Alfred Deakin, and Samuel Griffith, drafted the Constitution. After referendums, the Commonwealth of Australia was born on 1 January 1901. The Constitution created a federal system with the Washminster model (British responsible government + American federal features).
