Week 1 - Lesson 4: Referendums, Plebiscites and Changing the Constitution

Main Content: Changing the Rulebook

Lesson goal: Understand the difference between a referendum and a plebiscite, explain the double majority, and consider why Australia makes constitutional change difficult.

Deborah says: Ordinary laws can be changed by Parliament, but the Constitution is different. It is Australia’s rulebook for government, so Parliament cannot change it by itself.

Referendum

A referendum is a vote used to approve or reject a proposed change to the Australian Constitution. If it passes, the Constitution changes.

Double majority

To pass, a referendum usually needs a majority of voters across Australia and a majority of voters in at least four of the six states.

Plebiscite

A plebiscite asks voters for their opinion on an issue. It can guide Parliament or government, but it does not directly change the Constitution.

Why make change difficult?

The high threshold protects stability and makes sure big changes have broad support. It can also make reform hard to achieve.
